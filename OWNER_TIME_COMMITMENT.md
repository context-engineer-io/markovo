# Markovo: Owner Time Commitment Analysis

## The "Un-Automatable" 20% — What the Owner Still Does

Markovo automates roughly 80% of traditional agency work. The remaining 20% requires human judgment, authenticity, and decision-making. Here's what that actually looks like in practice.

## Weekly Time Budget

| Task | Frequency | Time Per Occurrence | Weekly Total |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Approving/rejecting AI-generated content** | Daily | 3-5 min (scan a feed, tap approve/reject) | ~20-30 min |
| **Recording a voice memo or jotting a content idea** | 1-2x/week | 5 min each | ~10 min |
| **Shooting raw photos/video on phone** | 1x/week | 15-20 min | ~15-20 min |
| **Responding to a flagged crisis or sensitive review** | Rare (1-2x/month) | 10-15 min | ~5 min (amortized) |
| **Attending a networking event / relationship building** | 1-2x/month | Already doing this | 0 (not new time) |
| **Reading the Morning Brief** | Daily | 1-2 min | ~10 min |
| **Quarterly strategy review (AI-generated, owner validates)** | 1x/quarter | 30 min | ~2 min (amortized) |
| **Answering occasional clarification from the system** | 1-2x/week | 2 min each | ~4 min |

### Realistic total: 60-90 minutes per week

That's roughly **10-15 minutes per day**, with some days being 3 minutes (just approving content and reading the brief) and others being 20-25 minutes (shooting a few product photos, reviewing a batch of social posts).

## Trust Curve Over Time

| Phase | Weekly Time | What's Happening |
|---|---|---|
| **Month 1-2** | 2-3 hours/week | Owner over-reviews, rewrites AI outputs, second-guesses strategy, calibrates voice. This is normal and healthy — it's how the system learns their preferences. |
| **Month 3-5** | 60-90 min/week | Trust builds. AI's brand voice improves from feedback loops. Owner settles into routine. |
| **Month 6+** | 30-60 min/week | Owner stops reviewing every post. System runs on auto-approve for trusted content types. Only intervenes on flagged items. |

## Compared to the Alternatives

| Approach | Weekly Time Commitment | Output Quality |
|---|---|---|
| **DIY marketing** | 8-15 hours/week | Poor to mediocre |
| **Managing an agency** | 2-4 hours/week | Good (but expensive) |
| **Doing nothing** | 0 hours | No results |
| **Using Markovo** | 1-1.5 hours/week | Good to very good |

## Value Proposition

The value isn't "zero time" — it's: **get agency-quality output for the time cost of checking your email.** The system does the thinking, creating, and executing. The owner just steers.

## Product Design Implications

The approval UI must be designed for **speed**, not depth:

- **Tinder-style swipe** on content (approve/reject/edit)
- **Batch approve** option for trusted content types
- **Auto-approve rules** ("always publish social posts that match my brand score threshold")
- **Escalation-only mode** where the system only surfaces items that need human judgment

The goal is to make that 10-15 minutes feel like 3 minutes. If the owner has to click into dashboards, navigate menus, and read long reports, the UX has failed. Everything should be actionable from a single feed — ideally from their phone.
